Introduction to AC Voltage Controller

AN AC Voltage controller converts fixed AC vVoltage directlt to a variable AC voltage at same frequency

AC Voltage Controller are of two types:

(1)Half-Wave AC Voltage Controller: Half-Wave AC Voltage Controller controlled in the half cycle of input AC voltage by varying the Firing angle.

(2)Half-Wave AC Voltage Controller: Full-Wave AC Voltage Controller controlled in the both half cycle of input AC voltage by varying the Firing angle.

Classification of AC Voltage Controller

Too see the details, move the mover over the labels/button

Half-Wave AC Controller: Single phase half wave AC voltage controller circuit using one SCR and one diode connected in parallel across each other in opposite direction used in most of the AC control applications. The AC power flow to the load can be controlled in the half cycles by varying firing angle. The RMS value of load voltage can be varied by varying firing angle. The half wave AC voltage controller is also referred to a uni- directional controller.

Full-Wave AC Voltage Controller: Single phase full wave AC voltage controller circuit using two SCRs or a single Triac is generally used in most of the AC control applications. The AC power flow to the load can be controlled in both half cycles by varying firing angle. The RMS value of load voltage can be varied by varying firing angle. The full wave AC voltage controller is also referred to a bi-directional controller.
